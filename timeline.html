Timeline

Discover Tetris's humble beginnings in the Soviet Union and its rise to worldwide stardom.

1984

Russian software engineer Alexey Pajitnov develops the very first version of Tetris on an old computer called the Electronika 60. The concept stemmed from his memories of playing with pentominoes, or a game where you use different shapes to create pictures, and how he found difficulty putting the pieces back in the box.

1986

The game is ported to the IBM PC and quickly spreads throughout the Soviet Union. The IBM had better graphic display than the Electronika 60, allowing for different colors to be shown for the pieces and a scoreboard.

1987

Tetris is launched on PCs in North America and Europe allowing for influence outside of the Soviet Union.

1988

Henk Rogers, a video game designer and publisher of Bullet-Proof Software, discovers Tetris during a Las Vegas tradeshow. He becomes instantly hooked on the game, gaining rights to release Tetris on the PC and Nintendo Entertainment System in Japan. Over two million copies are sold.

1989

After Rogers gains the handheld rights to Tetris, he licenses those rights to Nintendo, who then launch Tetris on the Game Boy. Due to its initial release being bundled with Tetris, over 35 million copies are sold, making it one of the best-selling games of all time.

1996

The Tetris Company is formed which becomes the source of all Tetris licenses. Guidelines are also established to make sure any iteration of the game is consistent with others in terms of gameplay and quality.

1997

Artist Roger Dean designs the logo for Tetris.

2002

Henk Rogers establishes Blue Lava Wireless to release Tetris on mobile devices in North America, soon becoming a leader in mobile games worldwide.

2005

Jamdat acquires Blue Lava Wireless. They obtain an exclusive, worldwide license lasting 15 years to release Tetris on mobile devices.

2006

As EA purchases Jamdat, Tetris is then released on the iPod and Nintendo DS, becoming Apple's #1 Mobile game and selling over two million copies worldwide, respectively.

2009

Tetris Friends is the first web-based Tetris game released. Sadly, this was later shut down 10 years after.
The game also celebrates its 25th anniversary!

2010

A new iteration simply named Tetris for the iPad is released, soon surpassing over 100 million paid mobile downloads, becoming the best-selling mobile game of all time.
Tetris merchandise such as clothing, jewelry, and household goods are sold.

2013

Tetris was added to NYC's Museum of Modern Art as a part of its exhibit on Applied Design.

2014

Tetris achieves more than 425 million paid mobile downloads.
A game was also played on the Cira Centre in Philadelphia, PA, winning a new Guinness World Record for "Largest Architectural Video Game Display."

2015

Tetris is inducted into the World Video Game Hall of Fame in Rochester, NY, and Pajitnov, the creator is awarded the prestigious Honorary Award at the Fun & Serious Games Festival in Bilbao, Spain. The game also wins another Guinness World Record for "Most Variants of a Video Game" with 215 different variants as of May 2015.

2018

Tetris Effect launches for the PlayStation 4 and PlayStationVR, earning more than 60 "Best of 2018" and "Game of the Year" honors.

2020

Apple announces Tetris: The Movie, a story about the legal rights for Tetris and the controversy surrounding it during the 1980s and 1990s.
