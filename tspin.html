T-Spin

T-Spinning is a spinning technique that can score a lot more points than a Tetris (four-line clear) can. This is also used in multiplayer to send garbage, or large chunks of gray blocks to another opponent. When going for a high score, heavy use of T-spins will often net you a new record, although it is fairly tricky to get used to.

T-Spin Double

  1. Form a T-shaped gap. In the GIF, the orange L-block and the green S-block on top with the blue J-block and straight piece on the side form a T-shaped hole.
  2. Soft drop the T-piece into the hole. Make sure to soft-drop (hold DOWN on the D-Pad) as hard dropping will just clear the very bottom line.
  3. Quickly rotate the piece to fit it. In the GIF, the piece is rotated left.

Note that for a T-Spin Single, the T-block above must be rotated right twice instead where the stem of the T fits into the green block. It has to be spun and to have left no gaps for it to count as such.

T-Spin Triple

One of the trickiest moves in Tetris is the T-Spin Triple as forming the gap to spin the piece into is usually difficult. The T-piece must be inserted under and then rotated either left or right. The sequence of the blocks on the right is usually a guaranteed way of scoring several back-to-back T-spin triples.
